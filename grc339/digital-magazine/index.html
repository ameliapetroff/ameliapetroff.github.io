Women at the Heart of Yosemite Climbing

Women have profoundly shaped Yosemite climbing, with pioneers like Lynn Hill breaking barriers and inspiring future generations. Their achievements highlight the vital role women play in advancing and redefining the sport.

Ropes versus Boulders

Rope climbing, especially on iconic big walls like El Capitan, involves long, multi-pitch routes requiring endurance and technical skill.

Lynn Hill

Lynn Hill's achievements in climbing epitomize female empowerment and inspire women globally. As a trailblazing climber, Hill broke barriers by becoming the first person, regardless of gender, to free climb the Nose of El Capitan in 1993.

Royal Robbins

Royal Robbins was a legendary American climber renowned for his pioneering ascents in Yosemite.

Warren Harding

Warren Harding was a trailblazing American climber known for his significant contributions to Yosemite’s climbing scene. In 1958, he completed the first ascent of The Nose on El Capitan, a feat that took 47 days.

Yosemite's Golden Age

The Golden Age of Yosemite climbing, spanning the 1950s to 1970s, marked a period of groundbreaking ascents and pioneering techniques.

Yosemite's Granite

Yosemite's rock is primarily granite, renowned for its durability and excellent climbing characteristics.

Awahneechee People

The Yosemite area was originally inhabited by the Ahwahneechee people, a part of the Southern Sierra Miwok.
